                            <b> ANC.DT.26 Calcium and vitamin A supplementation:</b> 
                            
                            <br/>
                            
                            <b>When:</b>
                            
                            <i>Named Event:</i> IF &quot;Population with low dietary calcium intake&quot; = TRUE AND/OR IF &quot;Population with vitamin A deficiency&quot; = TRUE
                            <br/>
                            
                            
                            
                            
                            <b>Then:</b>
                            <table class="grid-dict">
                                
                                <tr>
                                    <td>
                                        
                                        In populations with low dietary calcium intake, daily calcium supplementation (1.5–2.0 g oral elemental calcium) is recommended for pregnant women to reduce the risk of pre-eclampsia. 

Dietary counselling of pregnant women should promote adequate calcium intake through locally available, calcium-rich foods.

– Divide the total dose into three doses, preferably taken at meal-time.
– Iron and calcium should preferably be administered several hours apart rather than concomitantly.

Please refer to calcium sources listed below for additional guidance that can be provided. 
                                        
                                        <br/>
                                        
                                        
                                        <b>If:</b>
                                        
                                        <i>Applicability:</i>
                                        
                                        IF &quot;Population with low dietary calcium intake&quot; = TRUE
                                        
                                        <i>(Should Provide calcium supplementation)</i>
                                        <br/>
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        <b>Then:</b> <i>Additional sub-actions not displayed</i>
                                        
                                    </td>
                                </tr>
                                
                                <tr>
                                    <td>
                                        
                                        Dietary counselling of pregnant women should promote adequate calcium intake through locally available, calcium-rich foods.

Please refer to calcium sources listed below for additional guidance that can be provided. 
                                        
                                        <br/>
                                        
                                        
                                        <b>If:</b>
                                        
                                        <i>Applicability:</i>
                                        
                                        IF &quot;Population with low dietary calcium intake&quot; = FALSE
                                        
                                        <i>(Should Do not provide calcium supplementation)</i>
                                        <br/>
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                    </td>
                                </tr>
                                
                                <tr>
                                    <td>
                                        
                                        Give a dose of up to 10,000 IU vitamin A per day, or a weekly dose of up to 25,000 IU.

A single dose of a vitamin A supplement greater than 25,000 IU is not recommended as its safety is uncertain. Furthermore, a single dose of a vitamin A supplement greater than 25,000 IU might be teratogenic if consumed between day 15 and day 60 from conception.

Please refer to vitaminA sources listed below for additional guidance that can be provided. 
                                        
                                        <br/>
                                        
                                        
                                        <b>If:</b>
                                        
                                        <i>Applicability:</i>

                                        <i>(Should Provide vitamin A supplementation)</i>
                                        <br/>
                                        
                                        
                                        
                                        <b>Then:</b> <i>Additional sub-actions not displayed</i>
